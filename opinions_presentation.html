Expressing Opinions

Useful Phrases for English Conversations

Key Phrases

It resonates with me
Something deeply connects with your feelings or beliefs
I see eye to eye with you on...
You completely agree with someone
What's your take on that?
Asking for someone's opinion
Frankly / To be perfectly frank
Speaking honestly and directly

More Ways to Express Opinions

I'm inclined to think that...
A polite way to express your opinion while showing you've given it thought
From my perspective / From where I stand...
Introduces your viewpoint while acknowledging others may see things differently

Disagreeing Politely

I beg to differ
A formal, polite way to disagree with someone
That's a fair point, but...
Acknowledges the other person's opinion before presenting a contrasting view

Expressing Uncertainty & Personal Views

I'm of two minds about...
Expresses uncertainty or conflicting feelings about a topic
If you ask me... / The way I see it...
Casual phrases to introduce your personal opinion

Teaching Tips

Formality Levels:

"I beg to differ" is more formal, while "If you ask me" is casual

Agreement vs. Disagreement:

Mix phrases that show agreement with those for disagreement

Hedging Language:

Phrases like "I'm inclined to think" soften opinions, making them less direct

Conversation Flow:

"That's a fair point, but..." is excellent for respectful debates

Discussion Activity 1

Topic: Social Media

  • Do you think social media has a positive or negative impact on society?
  • Practice using: "From my perspective..." and "I'm inclined to think that..."
  • If you disagree with your partner, try: "I beg to differ" or "That's a fair point, but..."

Discussion Activity 2

Topic: Remote Work vs. Office Work

  • Which do you prefer and why?
  • Ask your partner: "What's your take on that?"
  • Express agreement with: "It resonates with me" or "I see eye to eye with you on..."
  • Share honestly using: "Frankly..." or "To be perfectly frank..."

Discussion Activity 3

Topic: Learning Languages

  • What's the best way to learn a new language?
  • If you're uncertain, use: "I'm of two minds about..."
  • Share your view with: "If you ask me..." or "The way I see it..."
  • Practice all the phrases you've learned today!

Summary

You've learned 10 phrases for expressing opinions!

Remember:

  • Use formal phrases in professional settings
  • Casual phrases work well with friends
  • Always be respectful when disagreeing
  • Practice makes perfect!

Keep practicing these phrases in your daily conversations!
